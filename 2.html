12-Tone Chromatic Scale

First Position

An Oberservation On Tuning

You may have noticed that we always play up to the 4th fret before reaching the next open string, except for on the B string. On the B string we play up to the 3rd fret before playing the next open string. This is because the interval between the B & High E strings is one half step lower than the interval between all other strings. This will present with some unique challenges down the road. Until then, just keep it in mind!

Left Hand Technique

It’s important to keep left hand fingers in a position where they are ready to play the note that is coming up next. You can almost let them hover over the next fret, or at make sure they're nearby. The tendency is often to pull them away from the fretboard, or let them move around in uncontrolled ways. You can see an example of this in the provided video. We’ll explore this more in the upcoming lessons.

Practice: Hearing First

Play a melody of your own, as simple or complex as you’d like. Try to really “hear” each note in your head before you try to play it on the guitar. If needed, sing it out first. Record this melody as a voice memo on your phone.
