Finding the Key of a Melody

Finding the key of a melody is like filling in missing pieces of a puzzle. You are given the notes contained in the melody, and must find where the interval pattern of the major scale (W, W, H, W, W, W, H) lines up over the melody notes. Sometimes this takes careful listening or guesswork, and sometimes you may recognize a pattern in the melody inherent to a given scale.

You might look for the root note of the scale by trying to find the most ‘settled’ sounding note. Most likely you've already played this note in the melody. Start by playing the melody again. After getting this in your ears, sing a note that you remember from the melody. Now from here, sing a lower note, and keep going down. You'll most likely find yourself singing the scale or most important notes of the key. Of these notes, try to determine which is the most settled sounding in relation to the melody.

If nothing else works, you can just start to play a major scale shape that you’re familiar with, moving it up by one fret at a time until you find one where every note seems to sit well with the song. This is your new palette of notes.
