Accordion • Capsule • About

Accordion is a web-based cloud storage platform with a focus on community and collaboration.

View clickable prototype.



The Problem

The world of boxes and clouds is one often ruled by business and monotony. Accordion was created in order to introduce a splash of color to that world. The issue that I set out to solve was one of creative collaboration. Many users are turned off by the idea of cloud services because they are often marketed for business use. These people often find themselves working in collaborative situations that end up in a mess of emails and texts. Accordion was designed to replace the collaborative workflow via its note taking & cloud-based server, keeping in mind a human element.

My Solution

Accordion’s solution is simple. A bold and colorful workflow-based approach to collaborative note taking for the everyday person. Accordion is a media-rich and responsive web app that keeps all of your project files and communication between collaborators within a single page.

UX Design Process

My process was developed through many rounds of research, prototyping, design, and user testing. Accordion’s branding was designed to be bold. A red box with bold white text. I found that many of our competitors used neutral branding, so I chose this as a point to stand out. Accordion is also highly collaborative. User surveys concluded this was the most sought after feature; not just a service but a workflow. I wanted to draw users from all backgrounds. I did this by designing a blog based around use of Accordion in the field. This aims to not only generate outside interest in Accordion, but to create a culture around it. Again, the goal is to humanize a web based service. In user testing, I had people of multiple age brackets try the product. This resulted in feedback that led us to make all buttons on Accordion text based (such as settings) and affirmed by a thick line that appears below your selection. In the same vein of ease-of-use, we decided to reaffirm the user’s location in the Accordion folder system in both the left column navigation, and the right side icon view. These two mirror each other so as to make the user comfortable with their location within the file system.

Reflections

I’m confident that Accordion could stand out on its own based on its own boldness and the human elements that I designed to be a part of it. I think in the future, making technology feel more personal and usable by any person or group will be important to my design process and it is what I felt most strongly about in designing Accordion. Furthermore, I wanted to create a product that made something like collaboration on a project actually feel fun.
