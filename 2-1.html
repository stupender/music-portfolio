Finding the Key of a Melody

Finding the key of a melody is like filling in missing pieces of a puzzle. You are given the notes contained in the melody, and must find where the interval pattern of the major scale (W, W, H, W, W, W, H) lines up over the melody notes. Sometimes this takes careful listening or guesswork, and sometimes you may recognize a pattern in the melody inherent to a given scale.

You might look for the root note of the scale by trying to find the most ‘settled’ sounding note. Remember there are only 12 notes to choose from, so don’t let the fretboard overwhelm you. Go back to the chromatic scale if you become overwhelmed by it. If nothing else works, you can just start to play a major scale shape that you’re familiar with, moving it up by one fret at a time until you find one where every note seems to sit well with the song. This is your new palette of notes.
