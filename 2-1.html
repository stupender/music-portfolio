Refining The Pallate

We most often play music in what’s called a key, or a decided set of notes. Choosing a key is like putting a filter over the 12 notes of the chromatic scale. We're now refining the pallate of notes that we will choose from. The key of a song is most commonly a major scale.

The major scale is a 7 note scale, made up of the intervals whole, whole, half, whole, whole, whole, half. A half step is one fret on the guitar, while a whole step is two. This series of whole and half steps can be started from any of the 12 notes in the chromatic scale, so we have 12 possible major keys. We're going to explore this in the examples below.
