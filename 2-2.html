Harmonizing Your Melody In Thirds

The most common harmony to a melody is a third above or below. It adds depth to the melody, and may help to reveal hidden colors inherent to the blossoming composition. Thirds can be played in two ways on the guitar: on the same string, or on adjacent strings.

A major third consists of an interval of 4 half steps (or frets)

A minor third consists of an interval of 3 half steps (or frets)

All String Sets

On the following page we’ll play these thirds on all possible string sets. If we’re moving thirds around on the same strings, we can just slide the shape up & down. When we’re switching strings it’s a bit more challenging. Because of the tuning of the guitar (mentioned on the previous page), we’ll have a different shape for major and minor thirds when we reach some of the higher string sets. It’s important to know & understand these all!
